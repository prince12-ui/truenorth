What Is Supported Living? A Complete Guide for Families and Individuals

Supported living is a care approach designed to help adults with complex needs live as independently as possible while receiving the right level of support. It focuses on dignity, choice, and personal development rather than institutional care.

At TrueNorth Supported Living, we believe that everyone deserves the opportunity to live a fulfilling and independent life, regardless of their challenges.

What Does Supported Living Mean?

Supported living allows individuals to live in their own home or shared accommodation while receiving tailored support. This support can include:

  • Help with daily living activities
  • Personal care
  • Medication support
  • Emotional and mental health support
  • Skill development for independence

Unlike traditional care settings, supported living puts the individual in control of their life decisions.

Who Can Benefit from Supported Living?

Supported living is ideal for adults aged 18 and above who may have:

  • Learning disabilities
  • Autism spectrum conditions
  • Mental health needs
  • Physical disabilities
  • Behaviours that may challenge

Each person receives a care plan tailored to their specific needs and goals.

The Importance of Person-Centred Care

A key principle of supported living is person-centred care. This means:

  • Support is designed around the individual
  • Personal preferences are respected
  • Goals are set collaboratively
  • Independence is actively encouraged

At TrueNorth, we ensure every individual has a voice in how their support is delivered.

How Supported Living Promotes Independence

Supported living helps individuals:

  • Build confidence
  • Learn life skills
  • Make their own choices
  • Participate in their community

This leads to improved wellbeing and a higher quality of life.

Why Choose TrueNorth Supported Living?

We are committed to:

  • Safe and high-quality care
  • Compassionate and trained staff
  • Respect for dignity and privacy
  • Continuous improvement

Our services are aligned with Care Quality Commission (CQC) standards to ensure the highest level of care.

Final Thoughts

Supported living is more than just care — it’s about empowerment, independence, and living with dignity. Choosing the right provider can make a significant difference in the quality of life for individuals and their families.

If you are exploring supported living options, TrueNorth is here to guide and support you every step of the way.

Understanding Person-Centred Care in Supported Living

Person-centred care is at the heart of modern supported living services. It ensures that every individual is treated as a unique person with their own preferences, goals, and needs, rather than simply focusing on their condition or diagnosis.

At TrueNorth Supported Living, person-centred care is not just a principle — it is the foundation of everything we do.

What Is Person-Centred Care?

Person-centred care means that support is designed around the individual. Instead of a “one-size-fits-all” approach, each person receives care that reflects their:

  • Personal preferences
  • Daily routines
  • Cultural background
  • Goals and aspirations
  • Level of independence

This approach gives individuals more control over their own lives.

Why Person-Centred Care Matters

Person-centred care is important because it:

  • Promotes dignity and respect
  • Encourages independence
  • Builds confidence and self-esteem
  • Improves overall wellbeing
  • Strengthens trust between staff and individuals

When people feel heard and valued, they are more likely to thrive.

How TrueNorth Delivers Person-Centred Support

At TrueNorth Supported Living, we implement person-centred care through:

1. Individual Support Plans

Each person has a tailored care plan that reflects their unique needs and goals.

2. Regular Reviews

Support plans are regularly reviewed and updated to reflect progress and changing needs.

3. Choice and Control

Individuals are actively involved in decisions about their daily lives and support.

4. Family and Advocate Involvement

We encourage collaboration with families, carers, and professionals.

Promoting Independence Through Care

Person-centred care is not about doing everything for someone — it is about enabling them to do things for themselves with the right level of support.

This includes:

  • Learning daily life skills
  • Managing personal routines
  • Building social connections
  • Developing confidence in decision-making

Our Commitment at TrueNorth

We are committed to delivering care that is:

  • Safe and respectful
  • Flexible and responsive
  • Focused on individual outcomes
  • Aligned with CQC standards

Every individual we support is treated with dignity, compassion, and respect.

Final Thoughts

Person-centred care transforms lives by shifting the focus from limitations to possibilities. It empowers individuals to live meaningful and independent lives.

At TrueNorth Supported Living, we believe that every person deserves support that recognises their uniqueness and helps them achieve their full potential.
